Top 10 Free Time Management Apps for Students

1. Google Calendar

Google Calendar helps students organize classes, study sessions, and events in a unified layout. Its time‑blocking interface lets you visualize your week, add reminders, and include video‑call links directly in events. Sync it across devices for seamless access regardless of location :contentReference[oaicite:73]{index=73}.

  • Drag‑and‑drop scheduling
  • Multi‑calendar support
  • Email/Meet Zoom link integration
  • Customizable reminders

2. Microsoft To Do

This intuitive task manager helps break assignments into manageable steps. With due dates, reminders, and shared lists, it’s a great tool for both solo and collaborative work, especially for students already using Microsoft 365 :contentReference[oaicite:74]{index=74}.

  • Sub‑task support
  • “My Day” task suggestions
  • Cross‑device syncing
  • Task list sharing

3. Todoist

Todoist relies on natural‑language task entry and prioritization. It supports shared projects, Kanban boards, and calendar integrations—ideal for managing coursework and group timelines :contentReference[oaicite:75]{index=75}.

  • “Buy bread tomorrow at 5 PM” style input
  • Labels, filters, priorities
  • Kanban & calendar views
  • Collaborative sharing

4. TickTick

TickTick merges task management, habit tracking, and Pomodoro timing. Tasks can have due dates and timers, while habits build into daily routines. A calendar view offers an overview of commitments :contentReference[oaicite:76]{index=76}.

  • Pomodoro timer
  • Sync across devices
  • Task + habit integration
  • Calendar overlay

5. Forest

Forest gamifies focus. Plant a virtual tree and let it grow while you work. If you leave, the tree dies—helping break phone addictions. Students love it for study sessions and distraction control :contentReference[oaicite:77]{index=77}.

  • Focus timer with growing tree
  • Distraction blocking
  • Real‑world tree planting
  • Progress tracking

6. Remember The Milk

Remember The Milk offers task lists with subtasks, priorities, tags, reminders, and sharing. Ideal for managing homework and collaborative assignments across devices :contentReference[oaicite:78]{index=78}.

  • Subtask structure
  • Smart reminders
  • Advanced sorting
  • List sharing

7. Clockify

Clockify is a time-tracker that helps students measure time spent on work versus breaks. Alerts for idle time and Pomodoro cues support focus and analysis of study habits :contentReference[oaicite:79]{index=79}.

  • Time‑tracking timers
  • Idle detection
  • Pomodoro reminders
  • Insightful reports

8. Toggl Track

Toggl Track allows students to start timers instantly and receives reminders to log time. Its free tier includes detailed reports and idle detection, helping refine time management skills :contentReference[oaicite:80]{index=80}.

  • One‑click time tracking
  • Idle reminders
  • Browser extensions
  • Usage reports

9. myHomework Student Planner

Specifically for students, myHomework tracks classes, assignments, and exams in a dedicated planner interface. Schedule view and reminders support homework management and academics :contentReference[oaicite:81]{index=81}.

  • Timetable-style layout
  • Assignment/exam tracking
  • Multi-platform sync
  • Custom reminders

10. Habitica

Habitica converts tasks into a game. Submitting “Dailies” earns XP and unlocks in-game rewards. Join guilds, complete challenges, and stay motivated in a social, RPG-style platform :contentReference[oaicite:82]{index=82}.

  • RPG-style tasks
  • Quests and social guilds
  • Habit/Daily/To‑Do system
  • Open-source with mobile/web apps
