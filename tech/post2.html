Top 10 Budget Smartphones Under ₹10000 in 2025

Explore the best affordable smartphones that deliver great value under ₹10,000

1. Realme Narzo N20A

The Realme Narzo N20A offers an amazing package for entry-level users. Powered by a MediaTek Helio G85 chipset and supported with 4GB RAM, it handles daily tasks with ease. Its 6.5-inch HD+ display is great for watching videos and reading content. The 5000mAh battery ensures that you get through your day without frequent recharges. On the camera front, it features a dual-lens setup with a 13MP main sensor. With Realme UI and Android 14, the software feels modern and fluid. It’s a solid pick for students and budget-conscious users.

2. Poco C55

Poco C55 is known for its stylish design and reliable performance. Featuring a 6.71-inch HD+ display and powered by the Helio G85 processor, it’s ideal for basic multitasking, web surfing, and media playback. The 5000mAh battery ensures long-lasting use, and its 50MP dual-camera setup captures decent photos in daylight. It comes with MIUI based on Android 13, offering customizations and app optimizations. At this price, it's one of the best value-for-money options for casual smartphone users and first-time buyers.

3. Infinix Hot 30i

The Infinix Hot 30i brings 8GB RAM at a surprisingly low price point, something rare in this budget. The Unisoc processor handles day-to-day activities decently, and the 128GB internal storage provides ample space for apps and media. With a large 6.6-inch display and a side-mounted fingerprint sensor, it feels premium. Its 5000mAh battery supports your daily usage without issue. This phone is excellent for students who need performance and space on a tight budget.

4. Lava Blaze 5G Lite

Lava Blaze 5G Lite is India’s most affordable 5G phone, proving that high-speed connectivity can come cheap. Powered by MediaTek Dimensity 6020 and 4GB RAM, it delivers smooth usage with support for 5G networks. It also features a clean stock Android UI, which enhances performance. The design is modern with a glass back feel, and the 5000mAh battery easily lasts more than a day. Ideal for those who want to future-proof their purchase on a budget.

5. Samsung Galaxy M04

Samsung Galaxy M04 brings the trusted Samsung brand experience under ₹10,000. It sports a MediaTek Helio P35 processor, 4GB RAM, and a 5000mAh battery. The One UI Core based on Android 13 makes daily tasks smooth and easy. With a 6.5-inch PLS LCD display and a dual camera setup, it is best suited for simple tasks like calls, WhatsApp, YouTube, and casual gaming. Great for elderly users or those who want brand assurance with basic use.

6. Itel P55

Itel P55 packs some serious features for its price. It offers a 90Hz refresh rate on its 6.6-inch display, which is great for smoother scrolling. With 6GB RAM and Unisoc T606 chipset, it can handle apps and media well. The phone comes with 128GB storage, making it a great multimedia device. The battery backup is excellent, and it supports Type-C charging. Perfect for students or first-time smartphone users who want speed and storage.

7. Motorola Moto E13

The Moto E13 comes with a stock Android experience, which means no bloatware or ads. It’s powered by Unisoc T606 and 4GB RAM, enough for daily apps and light tasks. The 5000mAh battery is long-lasting, and the sleek design makes it pocket-friendly. The highlight is its simple software experience and durability. It’s a smart pick for elders, children, or minimal users who want stability and clean performance on a budget.

8. Redmi A2+

Redmi A2+ is an ultra-budget phone that focuses on simplicity and reliability. It has a 6.52-inch HD+ display, Helio G36 processor, and 3GB RAM, which is enough for basic use like calls, browsing, and YouTube. The Android Go Edition ensures light resource usage and faster performance. It also features a fingerprint sensor, a rare find at this price. Best for beginners and first-time users looking for an efficient daily driver.

9. Micromax In 2B

Micromax In 2B is an Indian-made option that provides great value with a Unisoc T610 chipset and 4GB RAM. Its stock Android interface and 6.52-inch display make the experience smooth and clutter-free. It has a dual rear camera setup with decent image output in daylight and a reliable 5000mAh battery. It’s a patriotic choice for buyers wanting local manufacturing and clean software.

10. JioPhone Next (2025 Edition)

JioPhone Next continues to evolve in 2025 with better performance and improved features. Running on Pragati OS (customized Android), it ensures good integration with Jio services. The phone is powered by a Qualcomm chipset, supports voice assistance in Indian languages, and offers 4G connectivity. It's ideal for first-time smartphone users, elderly, and rural users looking for affordability and simplicity with all essential apps onboard.
